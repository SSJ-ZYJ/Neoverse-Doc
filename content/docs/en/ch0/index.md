## 1.1 Why This Document Was Created

In the current education system, many computer science freshmen lack basic computer knowledge and have hardly ever used or been exposed to computers. However, as of today, most universities do not teach this kind of foundational knowledge for various reasons. We do not want this lack of common knowledge to become a barrier to future learning and exploration in the field of computer science, which is why we authored this document.

## 1.2 What This Document Covers

## 1.3 Sources of Content

The document is primarily written based on the author's subjective experience and learning process. Theoretical and academic content is cross-referenced against widely recognized books and highly credible online resources, with AI tools used for supplementary proofreading. Despite this, unintentional omissions may still occur. If you find any issues or areas for improvement, please feel free to [**contact me**](mailto:hisos_shenshijun@outlook.com) with feedback.

## 3 Afterword

No matter what major you choose, interest is always one of the standards for judging whether your choice is right. Computer science, in particular, is an interest-driven discipline. If you have already spent some time on coursework and find that you have no interest in the relevant subjects or find learning them extremely difficult and torturous — then perhaps, in the author's opinion, continuing to struggle on this path is not a wise move.

People around you may talk about how "good" the career prospects in computer science are, or how "high" the salaries are after graduation. However, when you truly step into this field, you will discover whether the effort you put in is truly proportional to what you get — the obstacles, the difficulties, and the tension and hesitation at every critical decision — these are things they never mention.
